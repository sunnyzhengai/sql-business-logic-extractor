## Context

The content-site effort needs a publishing posture that is simultaneously (a) ambitious enough to build real thought leadership and an email list, (b) safe with respect to Yang's employer relationship, (c) consistent with the guidance about not monetizing the commercial brand prematurely and not using a commercial-brand website for methodology content, and (d) compatible with the fork-based content framework already captured in the wiki.

Yang surfaced a framing mid-brainstorm: "Thinking in Public" (TIP) as distinct from "Building in Public" (BIP). TIP shares the *why*, *problem*, and *market gap* — not the *how*. It is a stealth-founder posture: market validation and reputation-building without revealing IP, without triggering employer policies, and without requiring the disclosure paperwork that commercial-brand or product-launching content would.

## Decision

Adopt **Thinking in Public** as the publishing posture for the content site and all associated social-media activity for the duration of the pre-resignation period.

## Rules of engagement

1. **Industry-agnostic filter.** No healthcare-specific terminology in public content. Generic examples only (Orders, Customers, Invoices, Subscriptions, Memberships). No HEDIS, DSRIP, HHSC, Epic, Clarity, Patient A01. Those live in the internal wiki as source thinking; they do not leak to public surfaces.
2. **Problem-first narrative.** 90% problem, 10% high-level solution direction. Public articles establish the market gap without naming the product that fills it. Never mention the "engine," "parser," "extractor," or any commercial brand name in public content.
3. **Off-hours protocol.** LinkedIn and other employer-visible posting occurs outside work hours (early morning, lunch, evening). Removes any appearance of misusing employer time for side activity.
4. **Stealth reply to "are you selling something?"** Pre-prepared response: focused on current role, passionate about the problem space, exploring automation's role, would love to hear how the asker's team is tackling it. Neither a lie nor a sales pitch; opens the door for private follow-up (DM, email) without a formal product launch.
5. **Private follow-up asset layer.** Healthcare-specific detail (Epic INI-Item, HEDIS scenes, regulatory submission patterns) is used in private conversations — DM replies, email list follow-ups, direct prospect outreach — never in public posts. This turns the healthcare depth into conversion material rather than disclosure-triggering public content.

## Alternatives considered

**Building in Public (BIP) rejected.** Sharing commits, screenshots, architecture diagrams would reveal implementation, undermine IP protection, and look like "building a product" to employer — the exact posture the COI analysis flagged as high-risk.

**Private-only (no public writing) rejected.** Forfeits the market validation, audience building, and pre-resignation reputation work that TIP uniquely delivers. Waiting until post-employment loses 6–12 months of compounding.

**Commercial-brand public writing rejected.** Previously decided — branded content reverts to commercial posture and reintroduces the disclosure requirements TIP is designed to avoid.

## Consequences

- **Wiki–public two-layer separation.** The wiki stays healthcare-rich as private source thinking; public articles are translations that swap vocabulary and examples to industry-agnostic form. Same ideas, different skin.
- **Article lineup retuning.** The original 8-week lineup proposed earlier this session contains healthcare-explicit scenes (HEDIS, INI-Item) that need rewrites for TIP publication. The INI-Item deep-dive moves out of public lineup entirely and becomes a private follow-up asset for Epic-shop prospects.
- **Voice discipline.** "I've spent years watching this play out" — not "As a Senior BI Manager at..." — removes employer-identifying signal while preserving experiential authority.
- **Email list is the primary conversion surface.** Public content (LinkedIn, personal site) drives readers to sign up. Private follow-ups happen over email and DM. Site polls and email replies are the data that validates the market.
- **Market validation framing.** Publication is *market research*, not *marketing*. Low engagement = signal not to quit the day job. High engagement = de-risked founder runway. This framing reduces the emotional stakes of each post.
- **Publishing infrastructure implications.** Need a personal-brand domain (separate from any commercial-brand domain), static site with poll widget, email capture (Buttondown or equivalent), and a feedback-ingestion pipeline back into the wiki. See companion decision page on publishing automation architecture.
- **Re-disclosure trigger.** If at any point Yang commercializes (monetized skill, paid product, vendor listing, commercial-brand-named anything), the TIP posture ends and formal employer disclosure resumes. TIP is a pre-commercial posture, not a permanent one.
